# Mastery Learning

## Definition

Mastery learning (Bloom, 1968) holds that most students can achieve high levels of learning if given sufficient time and quality of instruction. The model uses formative assessment to diagnose gaps, corrective instruction to address them, and mastery standards to determine readiness to advance. Time is the variable; mastery is the constant.

## How To Apply It

1. Define mastery criteria (what counts as "mastered" — performance standard, not just score)
2. Teach the unit
3. Administer a formative check (diagnostic assessment)
4. Provide corrective instruction for those who haven't mastered
5. Reassess until mastery is reached
6. Advance to the next unit

## When To Use It / When Not To

**Use when:** competency-based adult learning, certification prep, safety training — any context where "everyone must reach the standard" matters more than "cover the material on schedule."

**Limitations:** requires high-quality diagnostics and corrective instruction capacity. Can be slow in time-constrained corporate settings. Needs clear, defensible mastery criteria.
