# Transfer of Training

## Definition

Baldwin & Ford (1988) define transfer as generalization of trained skills to the job context and maintenance of those skills over time. Three factor categories influence transfer: trainee characteristics, training design, and work environment. Barnett & Ceci (2002) provide a taxonomy clarifying dimensions of far transfer — confusion arises when transfer distance is unspecified.

## How To Apply It

- Design transfer deliberately: align practice cues to job contexts (encoding specificity) and ensure post-training supports exist.
- Measure transfer on multiple dimensions (near vs. far, time, context, function) rather than a single "behavior change" metric.
- Add job-embedded assignments within 1-2 weeks of instruction. Follow with spaced retrieval.
- Build transfer toolkits: checklists, decision aids, job aids used in the work system.
- Measure maintenance at 30/60/90 days.

## When To Use It / When Not To

**Use when:** learning is justified by performance outcomes. **Limitation:** transfer can fail even with strong instruction if the work environment blocks application (no opportunity, no supervisor support, competing priorities).
