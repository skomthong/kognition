# Kirkpatrick Evaluation Model

## Definition

The Kirkpatrick model (1959/1994) provides four levels of training evaluation: Reaction, Learning, Behavior, and Results. It is the most widely recognized evaluation framework in L&D and provides common stakeholder language for reporting.

## How To Apply It

| Level | Question | Methods | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Reaction** | Did learners like it? Was it relevant? | Surveys, feedback forms | Immediately after |
| **2. Learning** | Did they learn? Knowledge/skill gain? | Pre/post tests, performance tasks | During and after |
| **3. Behavior** | Did they apply it on the job? | Observation, supervisor reports, self-report | Weeks–months after |
| **4. Results** | Did it impact the organization? | Business metrics, ROI analysis | Months after |

## When To Use It / When Not To

**Use when:** reporting training impact to stakeholders who need simple, recognizable language. **Limitations:** often misapplied as linear causality (good Level 1 does NOT predict Level 3). Correlation between levels is weak. Level 4 attribution is extremely difficult without controlled designs. Phillips added Level 5 (ROI) but the attribution problem remains.
