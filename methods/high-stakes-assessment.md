# High-Stakes Assessment Design

## Goal

Design credentialing, certification, and licensure assessments that are valid, fair, and defensible — with explicit attention to consequences, equity, and privacy.

## Design Steps

1. **Build on testing standards** — use the *Standards for Educational and Psychological Testing* (2014, open access) as the professional anchor.
2. **Construct a validity argument** — Messick's unified view: integrate evidence about score meaning, use, and consequences.
3. **Align to competency models** — assessments measure defined competencies, not incidental knowledge.
4. **Ensure fairness** — test for differential functioning across subgroups. Provide accommodations that maintain validity. Audit for bias.
5. **Separate practice from credential decisions** — gamification and low-stakes retrieval practice belong in preparation.
6. **Address remote proctoring carefully** — systematic reviews highlight privacy, security, equity, and discrimination risks.

## Checklist

- [ ] Validity argument constructed (meaning, use, consequences)
- [ ] Aligned to defensible competency model
- [ ] Fairness audited (differential functioning, bias review)
- [ ] Accommodations available that maintain validity
- [ ] Practice environments separated from credential decisions
- [ ] Privacy impact assessed for all proctoring and data collection
- [ ] Remote proctoring ethics and equity reviewed

## Sources

- Messick, S. (1989). Validity. In R. L. Linn (Ed.), *Educational Measurement* (3rd ed.). Macmillan.
