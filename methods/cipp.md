# CIPP Evaluation Model

## Definition

The CIPP model (Stufflebeam) frames evaluation as decision support across the program lifecycle through four components: Context, Input, Process, and Product evaluation. Unlike Kirkpatrick's post-hoc levels, CIPP is designed to inform decisions at every stage — before, during, and after a program.

## How To Apply It

| Component | Question | When |
|---|---|---|
| **Context** | What needs exist? What problems? What opportunities? | Before design — needs assessment |
| **Input** | What resources, strategies, and designs are available? Which is best? | During design — comparing approaches |
| **Process** | Is the program being implemented as planned? What adjustments? | During delivery — formative monitoring |
| **Product** | What outcomes were achieved? Were needs met? | After delivery — impact assessment |

## When To Use It / When Not To

**Use when:** evaluating programs across their full lifecycle, especially for continuous improvement and stakeholder decision-making. **Limitations:** can be resource-intensive; requires evaluation capability at each stage. Better suited to ongoing programs than one-off training events.
