# Service Design Blueprint

## Summary

A service design blueprint maps the full learning experience across what the learner sees (frontstage), what the team does behind the scenes (backstage), and what systems and processes support everything (support layer). It reveals operational failures, handoff gaps, and friction points that instructional design models miss.

## How It Works

| Layer | What it maps | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Learner actions** (frontstage) | What the learner does at each touchpoint | Enrolls, logs in, watches video, submits assignment, attends session |
| **Frontstage interactions** | What the learner sees and interacts with | LMS interface, email notifications, facilitator, peer discussions |
| **Line of visibility** | — divider — | Learner cannot see below this line |
| **Backstage actions** | Staff/facilitator actions that support the experience | Facilitator reviews submissions, admin sends reminders, designer updates content |
| **Support processes** | Systems and infrastructure | LMS automation, xAPI tracking, calendar integrations, IT support, procurement |

## Key Principles

- **Blueprints reveal what journey maps can't**: a journey map shows the learner's experience; a blueprint shows the operations that make it possible (or fail to)
- **Map handoffs**: the most common failure points are handoffs — between enrollment and onboarding, between async content and live session, between learning and workplace application
- **Identify fail points**: mark where the process is most likely to break (technology failures, human bottlenecks, unclear responsibilities) and design contingencies
- **Use for multi-touchpoint programs**: a single e-learning module doesn't need a blueprint. A blended program with pre-work, live sessions, coaching, community, and post-program follow-up absolutely does.

## How To Practice This Skill

1. Pick an existing program and walk through it as if you were a learner — document every touchpoint
2. Then interview the team: what do they do at each stage? What systems support them? Where does it break?
3. Lay it out in the blueprint format (spreadsheet, whiteboard, or digital tool like Miro)
4. Identify the top 3 failure points and propose fixes
