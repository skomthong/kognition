# Heutagogy

## Definition

Heutagogy (Hase & Kenyon, 2000) is self-determined learning — extending beyond andragogy (self-directed) to contexts where the learner defines not just *how* to learn but *what* to learn and *why*. It emphasizes capability (the ability to act effectively in novel, unfamiliar situations) over competency (performing known tasks to standard).

## How It Works

- Andragogy → teacher sets curriculum, learner participates in planning
- Self-directed learning → learner takes initiative within a defined framework
- Heutagogy → learner determines the learning agenda itself; the role of the educator is to create environments that support emergence

Key features: learner-chosen pathways, double-loop learning (questioning assumptions and values, not just strategies), non-linear discovery, reflection on learning process itself.

## When To Use It / When Not To

**Use when:** the domain is complex, rapidly changing, and unpredictable — situations where competency models can't keep up. Professional development for senior practitioners, innovation-focused learning, doctoral-level inquiry.

**Limitations:** requires very high learner readiness and self-regulation. Without support, heutagogy can reduce coherence and produce unfocused exploration. Not appropriate for foundational skill-building where specific competencies must be demonstrated.
