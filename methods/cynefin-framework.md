# Cynefin Framework

## Definition

The Cynefin framework (Snowden & Boone, 2007) classifies situations into five domains based on the relationship between cause and effect — and prescribes different decision approaches for each. It prevents the common error of applying simple solutions to complex problems (or over-engineering simple ones).

## The Five Domains

| Domain | Cause-effect | Decision approach | Learning design implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Clear** (formerly Simple/Obvious) | Cause-effect is obvious to everyone | Sense → Categorize → Respond. Apply best practice. | Standard procedures, checklists, compliance training. [Mastery learning](mastery-learning.md) works here. |
| **Complicated** | Cause-effect is discoverable through analysis | Sense → Analyze → Respond. Apply good practice (expert analysis). | Analytical training, [CTA](../techniques/instructional/cognitive-task-analysis.md), expert-guided problem solving |
| **Complex** | Cause-effect is only understandable in retrospect | Probe → Sense → Respond. Safe-to-fail experiments. | [Simulations](simulations.md), scenario practice, [design-based research](design-based-research.md), emergent strategy |
| **Chaotic** | No perceivable cause-effect | Act → Sense → Respond. Stabilize first. | Crisis training, rapid decision practice, [OODA](ooda-loop.md) |
| **Confused/Disorder** | Don't know which domain you're in | Break the situation into parts; assign each to a domain | Meta-skill: situation assessment before action |

## Key Principles

- **Match your approach to the domain** — best practice in the Clear domain, expert analysis in the Complicated domain, experimentation in the Complex domain. Using Clear-domain thinking ("just follow the procedure") in Complex domains fails.
- **Most organizational learning problems are Complicated or Complex** — they involve human behavior, motivation, culture, and context. Simple "best practice" solutions rarely transfer.
- **Complex ≠ complicated** — complicated problems can be solved by experts with enough analysis. Complex problems have emergent, unpredictable dynamics that require experimentation and adaptation.
- **Learning design IS often Complex** — you're designing for human behavior in organizational systems. Iterative approaches (SAM, design-based research) are Cynefin-aligned for Complex-domain problems.
