# Action Mapping

## Definition

Action Mapping (Cathy Moore) is a performance-focused design method that starts with a measurable business goal, identifies what people need to *do* (not know) to achieve the goal, designs practice activities as the closest possible approximation of real performance, and adds information only as needed to support the practice.

## How To Apply It

Four steps:
1. **Identify the business goal**: What measurable outcome should improve? (Not "learners will understand X" but "reduce error rate by Y%")
2. **Identify what people need to DO**: What actions, decisions, and behaviors lead to the goal?
3. **Design practice activities**: Create the closest possible approximation of real-world performance — scenarios, decisions, simulations
4. **Add information only as needed**: Instead of front-loading content, provide reference materials that support the practice

## When To Use It / When Not To

**Use when:** the problem is workplace performance — sales, compliance, customer service, safety, leadership behavior. Action Mapping directly combats content-first design where the default is "put the SME's slides in the LMS."

**Limitations:** Less applicable to foundational education or exploratory learning where the goal is understanding rather than specific behavioral change. Requires access to real performance data and stakeholders who can define measurable goals.
