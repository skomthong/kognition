# Safety-Critical Training Design

## Goal

Design training for domains where errors have severe consequences (healthcare, aviation, industrial safety) — emphasizing safe practice, repeated simulation, structured debrief, and validated competence measures.

## Builds On

- [Dual-Process Models](../foundations/dual-process-models.md) — most safety failures are System 1 defaults under stress
- [Retention](../foundations/retention.md) — spaced retrieval for procedure maintenance
- [Habit Formation](../principles/habit-formation.md) — reporting habits built through stable cues and low friction

## Design Steps

1. **Use simulation with structured debrief** — high-fidelity simulations tied to effective learning (Issenberg et al. BEME review). Multiple sessions, not one-and-done.
2. **Assess both technical and non-technical skills** — communication, teamwork, situational awareness (CRM training evidence).
3. **Space practice over time** — distributed retrieval practice for procedures and decision rules.
4. **Train System 1 and System 2** — explicit rules and checklists (System 2) plus repeated practice until pattern recognition becomes automatic (System 1).
5. **Separate practice from credentialing** — gamification belongs in the practice layer. Credential decisions require reliability, fairness, and validity.
6. **Build habit formation for reporting** — embed near-miss and incident reporting into stable context cues. Reduce friction.

## Checklist

- [ ] Simulation used with structured debrief and multiple sessions
- [ ] Technical and non-technical skills both assessed
- [ ] Spaced retrieval practice scheduled for maintenance
- [ ] System 1 (automaticity) and System 2 (reasoning) both trained
- [ ] Practice environment separated from credential decisions
- [ ] Reporting habits designed with stable cues and low friction
- [ ] Competence measures validated (reliability, fairness)

## Sources

- Issenberg, S. B., et al. (2005). Features and uses of high-fidelity medical simulations. *Medical Teacher*, 27(1), 10-28.
- O'Connor, P., et al. (2008). Crew resource management training effectiveness. *International Journal of Aviation Psychology*, 18(4), 353-368.
