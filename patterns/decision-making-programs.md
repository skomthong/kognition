# Decision-Making Programs

## Goal

Design programs that improve the quality of professional judgment and decision-making — especially under uncertainty, time pressure, and complexity. This is not "teach decision theory" — it's building the PRACTICE of better decisions through structured experience, feedback, and debiasing.

## Program Architecture

### Module 1: Decision awareness
- How decisions actually work ([dual-process models](../../foundations/dual-process-models.md))
- Common biases and when they strike (anchoring, availability, overconfidence, sunk cost)
- Calibration exercises: "How confident are you?" vs actual accuracy

### Module 2: Decision frameworks
- [Cynefin](../../methods/cynefin-framework.md): match approach to complexity domain
- [Premortem](../../methods/premortem.md): anticipate failure before launch
- [Scenario planning](../../methods/scenario-planning.md): prepare for multiple futures
- Decision matrices and trade-off analysis ([decision visualization](../../techniques/visual/decision-visualization.md))

### Module 3: Practice under pressure
- Time-pressured [scenario-based decisions](../../techniques/narrative/scenario-based-learning.md)
- [OODA loop](../../methods/ooda-loop.md) drills with debrief
- Cases with incomplete information and competing stakeholder demands
- [Debrief](../../techniques/instructional/debriefing-techniques.md) focused on reasoning quality, not just outcome

### Module 4: Transfer and maintenance
- Decision journals: document real decisions, reasoning, and outcomes
- Spaced case reviews: revisit decisions after 30/60/90 days with outcome data
- Peer decision clinics: bring real decisions to a structured group for analysis
- [Coaching](../../techniques/instructional/coaching-techniques.md) on specific decision patterns

## Key Principles

- **Practice decisions, don't lecture about them** — reading about biases doesn't reduce them. Practicing decisions under realistic conditions with structured debrief does.
- **Debrief the reasoning, not the outcome** — a good decision can have a bad outcome (and vice versa). Evaluate the PROCESS: what cues were used? What alternatives were considered? What assumptions were made?
- **Calibration is the meta-skill** — knowing what you DON'T know is more valuable than being right. Build calibration exercises into every module.
- **Transfer requires ongoing practice** — decision quality decays without maintenance, just like any other skill. Build spaced practice and peer structures.

## AI-Augmented Decision Training

AI can enhance decision-making programs when used deliberately:
- **AI as scenario generator**: produce varied decision scenarios at scale, adjusting complexity and domain
- **AI as feedback provider**: immediate analysis of decision rationale ("You considered factors A and B but missed C")
- **AI as calibration partner**: present probabilities, track prediction accuracy, show calibration curves
- **AI as devil's advocate**: systematically challenge the learner's reasoning to surface blind spots
- **Human-AI calibration exercise**: learner makes a decision → AI makes the same decision → compare reasoning → debrief the differences

**Critical rule**: AI augments the PRACTICE, not the JUDGMENT. The goal is humans who make better decisions, not humans who defer to AI. See [Human-AI Teaming](../strategy/human-ai-teaming.md).
