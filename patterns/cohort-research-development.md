# Cohort-Based Research Development

## Goal

Design programs that develop research capability through cohort-based progression — supporting doctoral candidates, practitioner-researchers, and professionals building evidence-based practice skills. The cohort provides accountability, peer feedback, emotional support, and collective expertise that solo research journeys lack.

## Program Architecture

### Cohort structure
- **Fixed cohort** progressing through research milestones together
- **Peer triads or quads** for intensive feedback and accountability between sessions
- **Facilitated workshops** for methods training, writing clinics, and milestone reviews
- **Individual supervision** for personalized guidance on each person's research

### Milestone-based progression

| Phase | Activities | Cohort role |
|---|---|---|
| **Problem finding** | Literature exploration, gap identification, research question refinement | Peer critique of questions, exposure to diverse approaches |
| **Design** | Methodology selection, proposal development, ethics | Peer review of proposals, methods workshops |
| **Data collection** | Fieldwork, interviews, surveys, observation | Peer support during the lonely phase; troubleshooting clinics |
| **Analysis** | Coding, statistical analysis, interpretation | Analysis workshops; peer audit of coding/interpretation |
| **Writing** | Dissertation/thesis drafting, revision | Writing clinics, peer review, accountability deadlines |
| **Defense/completion** | Final preparation, presentation practice | Mock defenses, celebration |

## Key Principles

- **The cohort IS the intervention** — research is isolating. The cohort provides the social structure that sustains persistence through multi-year programs.
- **Psychological safety is critical** — sharing unfinished work, admitting confusion, and receiving critique requires trust. Build safety deliberately and early.
- **Methods training through practice, not lecture** — teach research methods BY doing research, with scaffolded support. Experiential learning cycle applies.
- **Writing is a skill, not a talent** — integrate [writing techniques](../../techniques/writing/) throughout, not as a separate "writing course." Literature review writing, analytical writing, and editing are core research skills.
- **Celebrate progress, not just completion** — multi-year programs need milestones with recognition. Proposal approval, data collection completion, first draft — each deserves acknowledgment.
