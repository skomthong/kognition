# Onboarding Program Design

## Goal

Design an onboarding program that transitions new hires from outsider to capable contributor — building competence, confidence, and belonging within the first 90 days while avoiding information overload and early disengagement.

## Design Principles

- **Don't front-load everything** — cognitive load on day 1 is already extreme (new environment, new people, new systems). Spread content over weeks using [spaced practice](../techniques/instructional/spaced-practice.md).
- **Prioritize by urgency** — what do they need NOW (safety, access, who to ask) vs. SOON (role procedures, tools) vs. LATER (strategy, culture depth)?
- **Build self-efficacy early** — design quick wins in the first week. Early mastery experiences predict long-term confidence and retention.
- **Social integration matters as much as content** — assign a buddy/mentor, facilitate introductions, create belonging. [Psychological safety](../principles/psychological-safety.md) starts day 1.
- **Situated learning** — move from observation to legitimate participation quickly. Real tasks (even small ones) beat content consumption.
- **Measure at 30/60/90 days** — not just satisfaction surveys. Can they perform core tasks? Have they built key relationships? Are they retained?

## Checklist

- [ ] Content spread across 90 days, not dumped in week 1
- [ ] First-day experience focuses on belonging and orientation, not compliance content
- [ ] Quick wins designed for the first week
- [ ] Buddy/mentor assigned before start date
- [ ] Spaced retrieval checks on critical procedures
- [ ] Real tasks assigned by week 2 (situated learning)
- [ ] 30/60/90 day check-ins measuring competence AND connection
- [ ] Transfer climate assessed (does the manager support the onboarding plan?)
