# Designing for Neurodivergent Learners

## Goal

Adapt instruction to reduce barriers for cognitively and perceptually diverse adult learners — not by pathologizing difference, but by building flexibility into design from the start.

## Builds On

- [Neurodiversity](../principles/neurodiversity.md) — the framing of cognitive difference as natural variation
- [Cognitive Load](../foundations/cognitive-load.md) — neurodivergent learners may experience load differently

## Design Steps

1. **Apply UDL from the start** — provide options for attention, pacing, and medium (text, audio, visual) while keeping learning goals constant. Do not retrofit accommodations.
2. **Offer flexible pathways** — allow learners to choose sequence, medium, and pacing where possible. Keep goals and assessment criteria consistent.
3. **Reduce sensory overload** — consistent navigation, clear contrast, minimal animation, predictable layouts. WCAG 2.2 provides technical criteria.
4. **Avoid anxiety-inducing mechanics** — no public leaderboards, timed assessments without accommodation options, or competitive framing in required training.
5. **Provide assessment accommodations** — alternative evidence pathways where validity is maintained.
6. **Test usability, not just compliance** — gather qualitative usability evidence from neurodivergent users.

## Checklist

- [ ] UDL principles applied (engagement, representation, action/expression)
- [ ] Multiple media options available for key content
- [ ] Flexible pacing and sequencing built in
- [ ] Assessment accommodations documented and available
- [ ] WCAG 2.2 criteria met for digital materials
- [ ] Usability tested with diverse learners
- [ ] Game/competitive elements are optional and cooperative
