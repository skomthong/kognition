# Designing for Low-Literacy Adults

## Goal

Design instruction that respects dignity and builds functional skills for adults with limited literacy or numeracy, using task-based learning anchored to real-world demands.

## Design Steps

1. **Use functional tasks** — anchor learning to real needs: reading instructions, filling out forms, interpreting workplace documents, understanding health information. Align to "functional literacy" framing from UNESCO SDG 4.6.1 and PIAAC.
2. **Provide multimodal supports** — audio, pictorial cues, and demonstrations alongside text. Ensure supports do not *replace* literacy practice where literacy is the explicit goal.
3. **Build dignity and psychological safety** — adult learners may carry past schooling trauma. Design early success experiences and a respectful climate.
4. **Use plain language** — clear, simple sentence structures. Provide glossaries for technical terms.
5. **Scaffold progressively** — begin with highly supported tasks and reduce support as competence grows.
6. **Measure with validated frameworks** — use adult skill frameworks aligned to PIAAC-like constructs.

## Checklist

- [ ] Learning tasks anchored to functional, real-world demands
- [ ] Multimodal supports provided (audio, visual, text)
- [ ] Plain language used throughout; glossary available
- [ ] Early success experiences designed in
- [ ] Progressive scaffolding with gradual fading
- [ ] Assessment uses validated adult skill frameworks
- [ ] Psychological safety and dignity are explicit design priorities
