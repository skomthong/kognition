# Cross-Cultural Learning Design

## Goal

Adapt adult learning design for cross-cultural contexts — localizing scenarios, participation norms, and examples while maintaining learning outcome integrity.

## Design Steps

1. **Use culture frameworks as hypothesis generators** — Hofstede's dimensions (power distance, uncertainty avoidance, individualism-collectivism) are starting points, not stereotypes. Validate locally.
2. **Localize scenarios, norms, and examples** — adapt cases, characters, and contexts to reflect learners' cultural realities.
3. **Provide multiple participation formats** — spoken, written, anonymous, synchronous, asynchronous. Reduce barriers from power distance and face-saving norms.
4. **Validate locally** — pilot with representative learners before scaling.
5. **Measure carefully** — compare participation and performance patterns by language, region, and role. Interpret differences carefully.

## Checklist

- [ ] Cultural dimensions used as hypotheses, not prescriptions
- [ ] Scenarios and examples localized with stakeholder input
- [ ] Multiple participation formats available
- [ ] Piloted with representative learners
- [ ] Performance patterns analyzed by subgroup with careful interpretation
