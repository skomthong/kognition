# Learning Operating Model

## Summary

A learning operating model defines HOW the L&D function is structured and operates — roles, processes, governance, technology, and partnerships. It's the organizational design question: not "what do we teach?" but "how is our learning function set up to deliver value at scale?" Without an explicit operating model, L&D defaults to reactive order-taking.

## Components

| Component | What it defines |
|---|---|
| **Strategic alignment** | How L&D priorities connect to business strategy and workforce planning |
| **Governance** | Who decides what gets built, what gets funded, what gets retired, what standards apply |
| **Roles and capabilities** | What skills does the L&D team need? Instructional design, learning technology, data analytics, facilitation, project management, content strategy |
| **Process model** | How work flows: intake → analysis → design → development → delivery → evaluation. ADDIE/SAM/agile as operating process. |
| **Technology stack** | LMS, LXP, authoring tools, analytics, xAPI/SCORM — the [systems layer](../systems/) |
| **Partnerships** | Internal (business units, HR, IT) and external (vendors, universities, consultants) |
| **Measurement** | How L&D demonstrates value: Kirkpatrick, business metrics, learner outcomes, efficiency metrics |

## Operating Model Types

| Type | Characteristics | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Centralized** | Single L&D team serves all business units; consistent standards; economies of scale | Consistency, governance, compliance-heavy industries |
| **Decentralized** | Each business unit has its own L&D; close to the business; responsive | Speed, business-specific relevance, autonomy |
| **Federated** | Central team sets standards, tools, and strategy; business units execute locally | Balance of consistency and responsiveness; most common at scale |
| **Center of Excellence** | Small expert team provides strategy, tooling, and quality; business units build most content | Scale with limited central resources; consulting model |

## Key Principles

- **Operating model follows strategy** — if the strategy is "reskill 5,000 people in 18 months," the operating model must support scale, speed, and measurement. If the strategy is "develop senior leaders," the model must support high-touch, facilitated, cohort-based programs.
- **Capability matters more than headcount** — a small team with strong ID, analytics, and facilitation skills outperforms a large team with only content production skills.
- **Technology enables but doesn't solve** — an LMS is infrastructure, not capability. The operating model must include the human skills to use the technology effectively.
- **Measure what matters** — completion rates measure activity, not impact. Operating models that report only completions will be funded like overhead, not like strategic capability.

## How To Practice

1. Map your current L&D operating model: who does what, with what tools, following what process, governed by whom? Where are the gaps?
2. Identify the top 3 bottlenecks in your current model. Are they capability, process, technology, or governance problems?
3. Benchmark: what operating model type best fits your organization's strategy and maturity?
