# NIST Privacy Framework

## Definition

The NIST Privacy Framework v1.0 (January 2020) provides a voluntary tool for managing privacy risk through enterprise risk management. It complements the NIST Cybersecurity Framework and provides a structure for organizations to identify, govern, control, communicate, and protect against privacy risks.

## How To Apply It

- Use for privacy risk management in learning systems: LMS, LRS, analytics dashboards, proctoring, GenAI tools
- Structure: Identify (what data, what risk) → Govern (policies, roles) → Control (technical/organizational measures) → Communicate (transparency, consent) → Protect (security)
- Apply before launching analytics, proctoring, nudges, or any behavioral tracking
- In many jurisdictions, supplement with GDPR or local legal requirements

## Key Principles

- Learning analytics are sensitive — minimize collection, define purpose, govern retention and access
- AI-enabled systems (GenAI, adaptive learning, proctoring) require explicit privacy governance
- Consent and transparency: learners should know what is collected, why, and how it's used
- Data minimization: collect only what serves a learning decision
