# SECTIONS Framework (Bates)

## Definition

SECTIONS (Bates) is a structured framework for selecting media and technologies for learning. The acronym: Students, Ease of use, Costs, Teaching and learning, Interaction, Organizational issues, Networking, Security and privacy. It emphasizes multi-criteria decision-making rather than choosing tools by novelty.

## How To Apply It

Use SECTIONS as a decision log and stakeholder alignment tool when evaluating platforms, tools, or media:
- **Students**: who are the learners? Access, devices, connectivity, digital literacy
- **Ease of use**: for learners AND instructors/facilitators
- **Costs**: development, licensing, maintenance, support, total cost of ownership
- **Teaching and learning**: does the tool support the pedagogical approach? (retrieval practice, collaboration, feedback)
- **Interaction**: what kinds of interaction does it enable? (learner-content, learner-instructor, learner-learner)
- **Organizational issues**: procurement, IT support, change management, faculty adoption
- **Networking**: does it support communities, collaboration, social learning?
- **Security and privacy**: data governance, WCAG accessibility, NIST PF alignment

## When To Use It / When Not To

**Use when:** making tool or media selection decisions for programs at scale. **Limitations:** SECTIONS is a structured decision aid, not a guarantee of effectiveness — instructional judgment is still required.
