# Universal Design for Learning (UDL)

## Definition

UDL (CAST) is a design framework to optimize learning for human variability by providing multiple means of engagement, representation, and action/expression. UDL Guidelines 2.2 is the established reference; UDL Guidelines 3.0 were released July 30, 2024 as an updated iteration.

## How To Apply It

Three principles:
- **Multiple means of engagement**: recruit interest, sustain effort and persistence, develop self-regulation options
- **Multiple means of representation**: provide options for perception, language/symbols, and comprehension
- **Multiple means of action/expression**: provide options for physical action, expression/communication, and executive function

## Key Principles

- UDL is proactive — design for variability from the start, not retrofit accommodations.
- Keep learning goals constant while providing flexible pathways to achieve them.
- UDL and WCAG are complementary: UDL provides the design philosophy, WCAG provides technical success criteria.
- UDL applies beyond disability — it benefits all learners dealing with cognitive load, time constraints, language barriers, and environmental distractions.
