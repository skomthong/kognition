# Online Course Formats (MOOC, SPOC, COOC, SOOC)

## Definition

Online course formats vary by scale, access, pedagogy, and social structure:

| Format | Full name | Scale | Access | Key characteristics |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **MOOC** | Massive Open Online Course | Thousands–millions | Open (free or freemium) | Content-heavy, low facilitation, high dropout, wide reach. Platforms: Coursera, edX, FutureLearn |
| **SPOC** | Small Private Online Course | Tens–hundreds | Restricted (enrolled cohort) | Cohort-based, facilitated, higher completion, more interaction. Essentially an online course with LMS structure |
| **COOC** | Corporate Open Online Course | Varies | Organization-internal or partner-restricted | MOOC-style content adapted for corporate learning; may include org-specific cases and assessments |
| **SOOC** | Small Open Online Course | Tens–hundreds | Open | Open access but small scale, enabling facilitation and community; bridges MOOC openness with SPOC interaction |

## Key Principles

- Format choice should follow learner analysis and learning objectives, not default to "MOOC" or "course."
- **MOOCs** excel at awareness, exposure, and self-directed skill-building at scale. They are poor at deep practice, feedback, and transfer without supplemental support.
- **SPOCs** support the social and teaching presence (CoI) that MOOCs lack. Better for professional development, certification prep, and cohort-based programs.
- **COOCs** adapt open content to organizational context — adding company-specific cases, assessments, and facilitation.
- **Completion rates** are misleading as quality metrics for MOOCs — many enrollees are browsers, not completers. Measure against intent.
- Transactional distance is highest in MOOCs (low dialogue, high structure) and lowest in SPOCs/SOOCs (more dialogue, flexible structure).

## Design Considerations

- For adult workplace learning: SPOCs and COOCs are usually more appropriate than MOOCs because they support facilitation, peer interaction, and transfer
- MOOCs work well for foundational knowledge that feeds into deeper SPOCs (funnel model)
- Social features (discussion, peer review, cohort identity) are critical differentiators — a MOOC with active social features behaves more like a SOOC
- Blended models: combine MOOC/SPOC content with live facilitated sessions for the best of both
