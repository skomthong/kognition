# WCAG 2.2

## Definition

Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2 is a W3C Recommendation (October 5, 2023) that defines success criteria for making web content accessible to people with disabilities. WCAG 2.2 extends 2.1 with nine additional success criteria strengthening usability for diverse users. Content conforming to 2.2 also conforms to 2.0 and 2.1.

## How To Apply It

Four principles (POUR):
- **Perceivable**: text alternatives, captions, sufficient contrast, adaptable content
- **Operable**: keyboard accessible, enough time, no seizure triggers, navigable
- **Understandable**: readable, predictable, input assistance
- **Robust**: compatible with assistive technologies

Three conformance levels: A (minimum), AA (standard target), AAA (enhanced).

## Key Principles

- Accessibility is a design constraint, not an afterthought. Integrate into "definition of done" and procurement requirements.
- WCAG compliance does not guarantee usability — test with actual users of assistive technologies.
- Section 508 ICT standards align with WCAG for many procurement contexts.
