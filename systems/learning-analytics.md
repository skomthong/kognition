# Learning Analytics

## Definition

Learning analytics is the measurement, collection, analysis, and reporting of data about learners and their contexts for the purpose of understanding and optimizing learning and the environments in which it occurs. For adult learning, analytics is most valuable when connected to decisions about support, pacing, and transfer — not vanity metrics.

## How To Apply It

- **Early warning**: detect at-risk learners (low quiz frequency, missed sessions, declining scores) and trigger support interventions
- **Retrieval practice tracking**: measure practice frequency, spacing patterns, and retention outcomes via xAPI
- **Engagement quality**: use [ICAP](../methods/icap.md)-coded activity audits (passive/active/constructive/interactive), not just click counts
- **Transfer evidence**: track post-program performance indicators when possible
- **Interpretive caution**: proxy metrics (logins, time-on-page) are unreliable indicators of learning

## Key Principles

- Analytics should serve learning decisions — if you can't act on the data, don't collect it
- Privacy governance: learning data is sensitive. Manage with NIST PF, minimize collection, communicate practices transparently
- Avoid surveillance framing: analytics for support, not punishment
