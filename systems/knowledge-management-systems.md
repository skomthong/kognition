# Knowledge Management Systems

## Summary

Knowledge management (KM) systems capture, organize, and distribute organizational knowledge so it's reusable rather than lost when people leave, change roles, or forget. In learning design, KM intersects with content operations, communities of practice, and the broader question: how does an organization KNOW what it knows?

## Types of Knowledge

| Type | Characteristics | KM approach |
|---|---|---|
| **Explicit** | Documented, codified, transferable in writing | Databases, wikis, playbooks, templates, documented procedures |
| **Tacit** | Experiential, intuitive, hard to articulate | Communities of practice, mentoring, storytelling, CTA interviews |
| **Procedural** | Know-how: how to do things | Job aids, checklists, video demonstrations, simulations |

**Key insight**: most valuable organizational knowledge is tacit — and it walks out the door every time someone leaves. KM systems that only capture documents miss the most important knowledge.

## KM System Components

| Component | Function | Tools |
|---|---|---|
| **Knowledge base / wiki** | Structured repository of documented knowledge | Confluence, Notion, SharePoint, MediaWiki |
| **Community platform** | Space for tacit knowledge exchange through discussion | Slack, Teams, Discourse, LMS forums |
| **Search and discovery** | Finding knowledge when needed | Enterprise search, tagging, metadata, AI-powered search |
| **Expertise location** | Finding the person who KNOWS, not just the document | People directories, skill profiles, social network analysis |
| **Capture workflows** | Turning experience into reusable knowledge | After-action reviews, exit interviews, case documentation, lessons learned |

## Key Principles

- **Capture at the moment of creation** — knowledge captured months later is degraded. Build capture into workflows: debrief templates, post-project reviews, standard documentation practices.
- **Findability > completeness** — a smaller, well-organized knowledge base beats a massive, unsearchable one. [Information architecture](information-architecture.md) is the critical enabler.
- **People > documents** — often the fastest path to knowledge is asking the right person, not searching a database. Expertise location systems complement document repositories.
- **Governance or decay** — unmanaged knowledge bases become graveyards. See [Content Operations](content-operations.md) for lifecycle management.
