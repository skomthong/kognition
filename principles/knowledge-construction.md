# Knowledge Construction vs Transmission

## Definition

The constructivist principle holds that learners actively BUILD knowledge by connecting new information to existing schemas, interpreting through their own experience, and generating personal meaning — rather than passively RECEIVING transmitted knowledge from an instructor. This is not a teaching method but an epistemological stance: knowledge is constructed by the learner, not delivered by the teacher.

## Two Models

| Dimension | Transmission model | Construction model |
|---|---|---|
| **Knowledge** | Fixed, external, transferable | Built, personal, contextual |
| **Learner role** | Receiver, container, audience | Builder, interpreter, agent |
| **Teacher role** | Deliverer, presenter, source | Facilitator, guide, architect |
| **Success measure** | Can the learner reproduce? | Can the learner use, transfer, create? |
| **Failure mode** | "I taught it but they didn't learn it" | "I facilitated but they built misconceptions" |
| **Design implication** | Content delivery, coverage, telling | Experience design, practice, meaning-making |

## Key Principles

- **Both models have value** — transmission works for simple factual information and efficient orientation. Construction works for deep understanding, transfer, and judgment. The error is DEFAULTING to transmission when construction is needed.
- **Constructivist alignment** (Biggs) — [Constructive Alignment](../methods/constructive-alignment.md) is explicitly built on constructivist epistemology: design activities that elicit the performances you want learners to construct.
- **Prior knowledge is the starting point** — constructivism explains why [learner analysis](../techniques/instructional/learner-analysis.md) matters: you can't build on a foundation you don't know exists.
- **Misconceptions are constructed knowledge** — they're not empty gaps but actively built (and resistant) alternative models. See [Schema and Mental Models](schema-and-mental-models.md).
- **Social constructivism** (Vygotsky) — knowledge is constructed through social interaction, not just individual processing. This grounds [collaborative learning](collaborative-learning.md), [CoI](community-of-inquiry.md), and [CoP](communities-of-practice.md).
- **Critical pedagogy adds power** — [Freire](critical-pedagogy.md) extends constructivism politically: knowledge construction is also consciousness-raising and liberation.

## The Practical Implication

The question is NOT "should I lecture or discuss?" — it's "what do I need the learner to CONSTRUCT, and what activities enable that construction?"

- If they need to construct procedural skill → practice with feedback
- If they need to construct conceptual understanding → explain, apply, compare, correct
- If they need to construct judgment → cases, scenarios, varied practice, debrief
- If they need to construct identity → reflection, community, meaningful experience
