# Cognitive Theory of Multimedia Learning (CTML)

## Definition

Mayer's Cognitive Theory of Multimedia Learning (2001/2009) proposes that people learn more deeply from words and pictures together than from words alone, provided design follows principles grounded in how the cognitive system processes information. CTML builds on three assumptions: dual channels (auditory/verbal and visual/pictorial), limited capacity (each channel can process limited information at once), and active processing (learners select, organize, and integrate incoming information).

## How It Works

Learning from multimedia occurs when the learner:
1. **Selects** relevant words and images from the incoming material
2. **Organizes** selected words into a verbal mental model and selected images into a pictorial mental model
3. **Integrates** the two models with each other and with prior knowledge from long-term memory

Design failures happen when any of these processes is disrupted — typically by extraneous load from poor layout, redundancy, or seductive details.

## Key Principles

| Principle | Description | Evidence level |
|---|---|---|
| **Coherence** | Remove extraneous material (seductive details, decorative images, extra text) | Empirical |
| **Signaling** | Highlight essential material with cues (arrows, bold, headings) | Empirical |
| **Redundancy** | Remove on-screen text that duplicates narration | Empirical |
| **Spatial contiguity** | Place corresponding words and pictures near each other | Empirical |
| **Temporal contiguity** | Present corresponding words and pictures simultaneously | Empirical |
| **Segmenting** | Break complex lessons into learner-paced segments | Empirical |
| **Pre-training** | Teach names and characteristics of key concepts before the lesson | Mixed |
| **Modality** | Present words as narration rather than on-screen text when paired with visuals | Mixed |
| **Multimedia** | Use words and pictures together rather than words alone | Empirical |
| **Personalization** | Use conversational style rather than formal style | Mixed |
