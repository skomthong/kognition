# Sensemaking

## Definition

Sensemaking (Weick, 1995) is how people structure the unknown — creating plausible interpretations of ambiguous situations so they can act. It's not about finding the "right" answer; it's about creating a WORKABLE story that enables action in the face of uncertainty.

## How It Works

Weick's seven properties of sensemaking:
1. **Grounded in identity** — who I am shapes what I notice and how I interpret it
2. **Retrospective** — we make sense of events AFTER they happen, not during
3. **Enactive** — our actions create the environment we then interpret (we don't just observe reality; we produce it)
4. **Social** — sensemaking happens in conversation, not in isolated heads
5. **Ongoing** — there's no starting or stopping point; we're always mid-sense
6. **Focused on extracted cues** — we notice a few elements and build a story from them (not everything)
7. **Driven by plausibility, not accuracy** — a plausible story that enables action beats an accurate analysis that produces paralysis

## Key Principles

- **Action precedes understanding** — in complex situations, you often can't understand until you act. This is why [probe → sense → respond](../methods/cynefin-framework.md) (Cynefin Complex domain) works better than analyze → plan → act.
- **Stories organize ambiguity** — sensemaking produces narratives, not analyses. "Here's what I think is happening" is a story, and stories are how organizations orient themselves.
- **The map is not the territory** — sensemaking creates useful maps (frameworks, narratives, mental models), but they are ALWAYS simplifications. Hold them lightly.
- **Sensemaking failure causes organizational failures** — Weick's analysis of Mann Gulch, Bhopal, and Tenerife shows that when sensemaking breaks down (loss of shared story, identity confusion, inability to update), catastrophe follows.

## Application to Learning Design

- **Facilitators are sensemakers** — a facilitator who can make sense of what's happening in a room (energy, confusion, resistance, breakthrough) and adjust in real time is practicing sensemaking.
- **Learning evaluation is sensemaking** — data doesn't interpret itself. Converting learning analytics into design decisions requires sensemaking.
- **Strategic narrative framing IS organizational sensemaking** — when a leader says "here's what I think is happening and what we should do," they're making sense for others.
