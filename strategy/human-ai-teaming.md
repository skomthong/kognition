# Human-AI Teaming

## Definition

Human-AI teaming is the design of work systems where humans and AI collaborate — each contributing what they do best. The strategic question is not "what can AI do?" but "what should humans and AI each do, and how should they interact?"

## Division of Labor

| AI does well | Humans do well |
|---|---|
| Speed at scale | Judgment in novel situations |
| Pattern recognition in large datasets | Ethical reasoning and values |
| Consistency (same quality at 3 AM) | Empathy and interpersonal nuance |
| Generating options and variations | Evaluating quality and relevance |
| Tireless practice partner | Adaptive facilitation |
| Instant feedback on structured tasks | Feedback on complex, ambiguous work |

## Key Risks

### Automation bias
Over-trusting AI output because it "came from the system." People accept AI recommendations uncritically, especially when they're busy, uncertain, or the AI has been reliable before. Mitigation: require human review, train critical evaluation of AI output, design friction into acceptance workflows.

### Cognitive offloading
Delegating thinking to AI rather than using AI to enhance thinking. If learners use AI to write their analysis, they don't learn to analyze. If designers use AI to skip instructional design thinking, design quality degrades. Mitigation: design workflows where AI assists but doesn't replace the cognitive work.

### Explainability
If the AI can't explain its recommendation, the human can't evaluate it. "The AI suggested this learner is at risk" — why? Based on what? Without explainability, human oversight is theater.

### Deskilling
If humans never practice what AI does for them, their skills atrophy. Over time, they become dependent on the AI and unable to function when it fails. Mitigation: rotate between AI-assisted and unassisted work; maintain skill through practice.

## Design Principles

- **Human-in-the-loop scales with stakes** — low stakes (practice quiz): AI alone is fine. High stakes (certification, employment decision): human review mandatory.
- **AI should increase human capability, not replace human judgment** — the goal is a human who makes BETTER decisions with AI support, not a human who rubber-stamps AI decisions.
- **Train the team, not just the individual** — learning to work WITH AI is a new skill. Include AI collaboration in training design.
- **Governance before deployment** — define: what decisions can AI make alone? What requires human review? What data flows through the system? Who is accountable when AI errs?
