# Validity and Reliability

## Definition

Validity is whether you're measuring what you think you're measuring — and whether the USES of the scores are justified. Reliability is whether the measurement is consistent. These are the foundational measurement concepts for anyone making decisions based on assessment data, learning analytics, or research evidence.

## Validity (Messick's unified framework)

Validity is NOT a property of the test — it's a property of the INTERPRETATION and USE of scores.

| Evidence type | Question | Method |
|---|---|---|
| **Content** | Does it cover the domain adequately? | Expert review, blueprint alignment |
| **Response process** | Are test-takers engaging as intended? | Think-alouds, process observation |
| **Internal structure** | Do the parts relate as theory predicts? | Factor analysis, item correlation |
| **Relations to other variables** | Does it correlate with what it should (and not with what it shouldn't)? | Convergent/discriminant evidence, criterion correlation |
| **Consequences** | Are the uses of scores fair and beneficial? | Impact analysis, adverse impact review, unintended consequences |

**Kane's argument-based approach**: build a validity ARGUMENT — a chain of claims and evidence. Define what you're claiming the score means → identify assumptions → collect evidence for each → address threats.

## Reliability

| Type | Question | Method |
|---|---|---|
| **Internal consistency** | Do items measure the same thing? | Cronbach's α, KR-20 |
| **Inter-rater** | Do raters agree? | Cohen's κ, ICC |
| **Test-retest** | Stable over time? | Correlation between administrations |
| **Parallel forms** | Equivalent forms produce equivalent scores? | Correlation between forms |

**Key relationship**: reliability is necessary but not sufficient for validity. A perfectly reliable test can consistently measure the wrong thing.

## Why This Matters Beyond Assessment

Validity and reliability thinking applies to ALL measurement in learning design:
- **Learning analytics**: are the metrics you're tracking actually measuring learning? (validity) Consistently? (reliability)
- **Surveys**: does your satisfaction survey measure satisfaction or just agreeableness?
- **Research evidence**: is the study measuring what it claims? Is the effect replicable?
- **Program evaluation**: do your outcome measures actually capture the outcomes that matter?
