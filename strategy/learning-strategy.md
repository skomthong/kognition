# Learning Strategy

## Definition

Learning strategy is the organizational-level plan that aligns learning investments with business strategy — deciding WHAT to build, for WHOM, at what SCALE, with what RESOURCES, measured by what OUTCOMES. It's the bridge between business goals and the [learning operating model](../systems/learning-operating-model.md) that executes.

## Components

| Component | Question it answers |
|---|---|
| **Strategic alignment** | What business outcomes does learning need to support? |
| **Capability priorities** | What capabilities are most critical? Where are the biggest gaps? |
| **Program portfolio** | What mix of programs, formats, and delivery modes? |
| **Audience segmentation** | Who needs what depth? (Tiered delivery) |
| **Resource allocation** | Where to invest: build, buy, partner? |
| **Measurement framework** | How will we know if it's working? (Beyond completion) |
| **Technology strategy** | What infrastructure enables the strategy? |
| **Governance** | Who decides what gets built, funded, retired? |
| **Change management** | How will we drive adoption and behavior change? |

## Program Portfolio Thinking

Not every learning need requires the same investment:

| Investment level | When | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **High** (custom, intensive) | Strategic capability, competitive advantage, high-risk compliance | Leadership development, safety-critical training, reskilling programs |
| **Medium** (blended, cohort) | Role-specific competency, professional development | Management skills, technical upskilling, certification prep |
| **Low** (curated, self-serve) | Awareness, information, personal development | Microlearning libraries, OER curation, self-paced refreshers |

## Key Principles

- **Strategy before programs** — building programs without strategy produces a random collection of training, not a capability system
- **Business outcomes, not learning metrics** — strategy is justified by business impact (performance, retention, revenue, risk reduction), not completion rates
- **Portfolio balance** — overinvesting in awareness (low-cost, low-impact) while underinvesting in strategic capability (high-cost, high-impact) is the most common portfolio error
- **Governance prevents drift** — without governance, programs accumulate based on who asks loudest, not what matters most
- **Strategy is living** — review annually against business strategy shifts, workforce changes, and technology evolution
