# Trust Calibration

## Definition

Trust calibration is the skill of appropriately calibrating how much trust to place in a source, tool, system, or person — neither over-trusting (blind acceptance) nor under-trusting (paralytic skepticism). It extends [trust and credibility](../principles/trust-and-credibility.md) from a communication principle to a strategic judgment skill.

## The Calibration Problem

| Error | What happens | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| **Over-trust** | Accept uncritically — AI output, expert opinion, research finding, data dashboard | Act on flawed information; automation bias; unquestioned authority |
| **Under-trust** | Reject everything — dismiss AI, ignore data, distrust experts | Analysis paralysis; reinvent the wheel; miss valid insights |
| **Miscalibrated trust** | Trust the wrong things highly, distrust the right things | Anecdote overrides data; charismatic speaker overrides evidence; flashy visualization overrides careful analysis |

## Calibration Framework

For any source, evaluate:

| Dimension | Question | Higher trust when... |
|---|---|---|
| **Track record** | Has this source been reliable before? | Consistent accuracy over time |
| **Transparency** | Can I see the methods/reasoning? | Methods visible, assumptions stated |
| **Alignment** | Does the source have incentives that align with accuracy? | No conflicts of interest |
| **Corroboration** | Do independent sources agree? | Multiple independent confirmations |
| **Uncertainty disclosure** | Does the source acknowledge limits? | Honest about what it doesn't know |
| **Updatability** | Does the source update when new evidence emerges? | Responsive to correction |

## Application

### Trusting AI
- New AI tool: start with low trust, verify outputs, build trust through observed accuracy
- Established AI with good track record: moderate trust, spot-check periodically
- High-stakes decision: always verify regardless of trust level

### Trusting research
- Single study: interesting but insufficient. Meta-analysis: much stronger.
- Industry-funded research: check for independence. Replicated findings: high trust.
- "Best practice" claims without evidence: low trust.

### Trusting your own judgment
- [Metacognition](../principles/metacognition.md): monitor your confidence calibration
- High expertise in this domain: trust intuition more (Pattern recognition is System 1 expertise)
- Low expertise or novel domain: trust structured processes more (checklists, frameworks, peer review)
