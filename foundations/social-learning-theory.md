# Social Learning Theory (Bandura)

## Definition

Bandura's social learning theory (1977) emphasizes learning through modeling and reciprocal determinism — behavior, personal factors, and environment interact continuously. Observational learning depends on four processes: attention to the model, retention of observed behavior, motor reproduction, and motivation to reproduce.

## Key Principles

- Adults learn from observing credible models in realistic contexts. Expert demonstrations, video exemplars, and peer modeling are direct applications.
- Reciprocal determinism means the environment shapes behavior AND behavior shapes the environment — design must account for both.
- Observation plus opportunity to encode and rehearse can precede performance — "watch, then practice, then get feedback" is a valid sequence.
- Social learning requires credible models and psychological safety. If the model is not perceived as relevant or competent, observation doesn't produce learning.
