# Leadership Communication

## Summary

Leadership communication is communication that creates clarity, alignment, and action across organizational levels. It combines the [pyramid principle](../writing/pyramid-principle.md) (lead with the point), [rhetoric](../writing/rhetoric.md) (ethos, pathos, logos), and audience awareness to communicate effectively upward (executives), across (peers), and downward (teams).

## Communication Directions

### Communicating UP (to executives/decision-makers)
- **Lead with the recommendation or decision needed** — executives don't have time for build-up
- **Frame the business impact** — connect to outcomes they care about (revenue, risk, reputation, speed)
- **Anticipate questions** — prepare for "How much?" "By when?" "What's the risk?" "What are the alternatives?"
- **One page max** — for initial communication. Details available on request.

### Communicating ACROSS (to peers/partners)
- **Frame as shared problem** — "We both need X, here's how we can get there together"
- **Respect their expertise** — don't explain their domain to them
- **Be explicit about what you need** — don't hint. "I need your team to review by Friday" is clearer than "It would be great if we could get some feedback soon."

### Communicating DOWN (to teams)
- **Explain the WHY** — not just what to do, but why it matters and how it connects to the bigger picture
- **Be honest about uncertainty** — "Here's what I know, here's what I don't, here's what we're doing about it"
- **Create psychological safety** — invite questions, admit what you got wrong, respond to concerns without dismissal
- **Follow up** — communication isn't complete when you send it; it's complete when the team acts on it

## Framing for Decision-Makers

How you frame a message determines what response you get:
- **Problem frame**: "We have a retention problem — 60% of training content is forgotten within a month"
- **Opportunity frame**: "By adding spaced practice, we can increase 90-day retention by 40%"
- **Risk frame**: "Without follow-up, the $500K training investment has a shelf life of 4 weeks"

Same facts, different frames, different responses. Choose the frame that matches your audience's motivational pattern.

## How To Practice

1. Write a one-paragraph recommendation to an executive. Does it lead with the point? Does it frame the business impact?
2. Rewrite the same message for a peer team. What changes?
3. Rewrite it again as a team communication. What WHY do you add?
