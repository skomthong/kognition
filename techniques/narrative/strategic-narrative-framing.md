# Strategic Narrative Framing

## Summary

Strategic narrative framing is the deliberate construction of a STORY about what's happening, why it matters, and what should be done — used by leaders, designers, and change agents to shape how people interpret situations and mobilize action. It's where [framing effects](../../principles/framing-effects.md) meet [rhetoric](../writing/rhetoric.md) meet [leadership communication](leadership-communication.md) at the level of organizational meaning-making.

## How It Works

A strategic narrative answers three questions:
1. **Where are we?** — the current reality, honestly stated
2. **Where are we going?** — the desired future state
3. **How do we get there?** — the path, the roles, and the first steps

The narrative is STRATEGIC because it selects, emphasizes, and sequences information to create a specific interpretation — not to deceive, but to provide coherence and direction in complex, ambiguous situations.

## Framing Moves

| Move | What it does | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Problem framing** | Defines WHAT we're solving (and implicitly, what we're not) | "This is a retention problem, not an engagement problem" — shifts from gamification to spacing |
| **Opportunity framing** | Repositions a threat as a chance | "AI isn't replacing L&D — it's freeing us to focus on what humans do best: facilitation, coaching, judgment" |
| **Identity framing** | Connects the initiative to who we ARE | "We're a learning organization — this is what learning organizations do" |
| **Urgency framing** | Creates a case for action NOW | "Every month without spacing costs us $X in retraining" (uses [loss aversion](../../principles/framing-effects.md)) |
| **Progress framing** | Shows momentum to sustain commitment | "The pilot showed 40% retention improvement — we're scaling what works" |
| **Reframing** | Shifts an existing narrative to a new interpretation | "We've been calling this 'training' but it's really 'performance support' — and that changes everything about how we design it" |

## Key Principles

- **Narrative is not spin** — strategic framing should be grounded in evidence and honest about uncertainty. The goal is coherence and direction, not manipulation.
- **The frame you choose excludes other frames** — "retention problem" excludes "culture problem." Be aware of what your frame makes invisible and address it explicitly.
- **Competing narratives are normal** — stakeholders have their own frames. Effective framing acknowledges and integrates competing narratives rather than dismissing them.
- **Narrative + evidence is stronger than either alone** — a story without data is anecdote; data without narrative is ignored.

## How To Practice

1. Take a current initiative and write the 3-sentence strategic narrative (where we are, where we're going, how we get there).
2. Reframe the same situation from two different angles (problem frame vs. opportunity frame). Notice how the implied action changes.
3. Identify the dominant narrative in your organization about learning. Is it accurate? What would a reframe look like?
