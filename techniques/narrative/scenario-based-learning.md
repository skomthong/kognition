# Scenario-Based Learning

## Summary

Scenario-based learning (SBL) places learners in realistic situations requiring decisions under constraints — with consequence feedback and reflective debrief. It includes branching narratives, character-driven cases, and simulation-based decision practice.

## The Science

- Based on: [Narrative Cognition](../foundations/narrative-cognition.md) — stories provide retrieval scaffolds and situated meaning; [Dual-Process Models](../foundations/dual-process-models.md) — scenarios train both deliberate reasoning (System 2) and pattern recognition (System 1)
- Related: [Behavior Change](../principles/behavior-change.md) — practicing decisions in consequence-rich contexts builds behavioral readiness; [Retention](../foundations/retention.md) — contextually embedded decisions are retained more durably
- [Cognitive Load](../foundations/cognitive-load.md) — scenarios must be scaffolded for novices

## How To Apply It

**Core structure:** Realistic setting -> role constraints -> decision points -> consequence feedback -> reflective debrief.

**Branching narratives:** Decision nodes with feedback and consequences. Use "bounded branching" — meaningful branches that converge via "foldback." Risk: learners may game branches; mitigate with reflection prompts and delayed consequences.

**Character-driven cases:** Protagonist whose constraints match learner constraints (time pressure, stakeholder conflict). Align emotional stakes to relevance, not melodrama.

**Reflective prompts:** Ask for decision rationales: "What did you notice, what did you assume, what did you trade off?" Embed consequences that mirror real systems, then debrief.

## When To Use It / When Not To

**Use when:**
- Learning involves judgment, decision-making, or professional identity formation
- Authentic practice is risky, expensive, or rare
- Pattern recognition for complex, ambiguous situations is needed

**Use with caution when:**
- Scenarios are poorly scaffolded for novices (overload risk)
- Branching complexity exceeds authoring capacity
- Narrative immersion is not paired with critical analysis
