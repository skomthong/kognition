# Entertainment-Education

## Summary

Entertainment-education (edutainment) is the deliberate design of media messages to both entertain and educate — increasing knowledge, shaping attitudes, and changing behavior. Singhal & Rogers describe it as a designed social change communication strategy with global applications.

## The Science

- Based on: [Narrative Cognition](../foundations/narrative-cognition.md) — narrative immersion (transportation) is the primary persuasive mechanism
- Related: [Behavior Change](../principles/behavior-change.md) — targets attitude and behavior change at population scale through modeled behavior and social learning

## How To Apply It

- Design drama episodes, documentary formats, or serial narratives around a specific behavior change or knowledge goal.
- Use modeled behavior — characters demonstrate desired actions and experience realistic consequences.
- Pair with critical reflection prompts and evidence checks.
- Use when audience scale justifies production investment.

## When To Use It / When Not To

**Use when:**
- Behavior or attitude change is the primary goal at population scale
- Narrative immersion serves as a pathway to belief or behavioral shift

**Use with caution when:**
- Intent is not transparent — ethics require disclosure
- Content risks stigmatizing groups
- Persuasion replaces critical thinking
