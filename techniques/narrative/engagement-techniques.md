# Engagement Techniques

## Summary

Engagement techniques are specific moves that capture and sustain the audience's attention and involvement. They work by triggering [curiosity](../../foundations/curiosity.md) (information gaps), leveraging [narrative cognition](../../foundations/narrative-cognition.md) (stories and meaning), and managing [attention](../../foundations/attention.md) (novelty and relevance).

## Technique Catalog

### Hooks (opening moves)
| Technique | Example | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| **Startling statistic** | "Within one month, your learners will forget 80% of what you just taught them." | Creates urgency through information gap |
| **Provocative question** | "What if everything you know about engagement is wrong?" | Triggers curiosity, challenges assumptions |
| **In medias res** | "It's 4:47 PM. Your phone buzzes. It's the client." | Immediate immersion, narrative hook |
| **Contradiction** | "The harder the practice, the better the learning — and the worse it feels." | Cognitive conflict demands resolution |
| **Personal stake** | "Think about the last training you attended. How much do you remember?" | Connects to the reader's own experience |

### Sustaining techniques
| Technique | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Examples and mini-cases** | Make abstract concepts concrete and memorable |
| **Analogies** | Bridge unfamiliar concepts to familiar ones ([Metaphor and Analogy](../writing/metaphor-and-analogy.md)) |
| **Rhetorical questions** | Create micro-information-gaps that keep the reader actively processing |
| **Pattern interrupts** | Break monotony with a shift in format, tone, or medium |
| **Callbacks** | Reference an earlier example or story — creates coherence and rewards attention |
| **Pacing variation** | Alternate between dense analysis and light examples; between long sections and short ones |
| **Direct address** | "Here's why this matters for YOUR next design" — personalizes relevance |

### Closing techniques
| Technique | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Callback to opening** | Return to the opening hook — creates closure and symmetry |
| **Call to action** | Specific next step: "Try this in your next module" |
| **Resonant image** | End with a vivid, memorable phrase or scene |
| **Open question** | Leave the audience thinking: "So the real question is..." |

## Key Principles

- **Engagement is not entertainment** — the goal is cognitive involvement, not amusement. An engaged learner is THINKING, not just smiling.
- **Every technique must serve the learning goal** — a great hook that leads nowhere is a seductive detail.
- **Vary the techniques** — the same move repeated loses impact. Alternate between questions, examples, stories, and data.
- **[ARCS](../../principles/arcs.md) provides the diagnostic** — Attention (hooks), Relevance (examples connected to their work), Confidence (achievable practice), Satisfaction (meaningful application).

## How To Practice

1. Write 3 different hooks for the same content. Which one would make YOU keep reading?
2. Take an abstract paragraph and add one example, one analogy, and one rhetorical question. Notice how the energy changes.
3. End a piece of writing with a callback to its opening. Does it create a sense of completion?
