# Transmedia Storytelling

## Summary

Transmedia storytelling (Jenkins) disperses integral elements of a narrative systematically across multiple delivery channels — each medium contributing uniquely to a unified experience. In learning design, a case or scenario unfolds across video, documents, forums, and interactive simulation.

## The Science

- Based on: [Narrative Cognition](../foundations/narrative-cognition.md) — multiple modalities engage different cognitive pathways and provide richer context
- Related: [Retention](../foundations/retention.md) — multiple encoding pathways strengthen memory traces; [Behavior Change](../principles/behavior-change.md) — sustained engagement across channels reinforces shifts

## How To Apply It

- A leadership case unfolds via short video scenes, emails/documents, forum debate, and a capstone simulation. Each medium contributes uniquely.
- Use in flagship programs where immersion and identity matter enough to justify production costs.

## When To Use It / When Not To

**Use when:**
- The experience benefits from sustained immersion and multiple perspectives
- Multiple media channels are already available
- Identity formation or complex sensemaking are goals

**Use with caution when:**
- Production cost exceeds learning value
- Accessibility cannot be guaranteed across all channels
- Privacy controls cannot be maintained consistently
