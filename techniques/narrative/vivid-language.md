# Vivid Language

## Summary

Vivid language creates mental images — it makes abstract ideas concrete and emotionally resonant through sensory detail, specific imagery, and "show don't tell" technique. It works because it activates [dual coding](../../principles/dual-coding.md) (visual + verbal processing) and because concrete information is more memorable than abstract information.

## Techniques

### Sensory details
Engage the reader's senses — what can they see, hear, feel?
- *Abstract:* "The work environment was stressful."
- *Vivid:* "The open-plan office hummed with overlapping phone calls. A Slack notification pinged every 30 seconds. His coffee had gone cold an hour ago."

### Concrete over abstract
Replace general statements with specific instances:
- *Abstract:* "Performance declined."
- *Concrete:* "Three projects shipped late in two months. Two clients escalated to the VP."

### Show, don't tell
Instead of labeling (telling), present the evidence and let the reader conclude:
- *Telling:* "She was an ineffective manager."
- *Showing:* "In six months, three of her five team members had requested transfers."

### Emotional precision
Name the specific emotion, not a vague category:
- *Vague:* "He felt bad."
- *Precise:* "He felt the slow heat of embarrassment rising from his collar." or simply: "He dreaded the next meeting."

## When To Use It

- **Scenario openings** — hook with a vivid moment, not a summary
- **Key messages** — one vivid phrase is more memorable than a paragraph of abstract explanation
- **Case details** — specific, concrete details create the realistic texture that makes cases believable
- **Facilitator language** — vivid examples in debrief make abstract principles sticky

## When NOT To Use It

- **Data presentation** — let the numbers speak; don't embroider them with imagery
- **Procedures and instructions** — clarity and directness over vivid description
- **When it distracts** — vivid details that are irrelevant to the learning goal are seductive details (CTML) that increase extraneous load

## How To Practice

1. Rewrite an abstract paragraph using only concrete, sensory details.
2. Take a "telling" sentence and convert it to "showing" — present the evidence instead of the label.
3. Describe a common workplace moment in 50 words using at least 3 senses.
