# Executive Summary Writing

## Summary

An executive summary is a self-contained document that conveys the full value of a longer report, proposal, or analysis in a fraction of the length. A decision-maker who reads ONLY the executive summary should have everything they need to decide or act. It is the single highest-leverage piece of professional writing.

## Structure

| Section | Content | Length |
|---|---|---|
| **Context** | What this is about, why now, why it matters | 1-2 sentences |
| **Key finding or recommendation** | The answer, upfront ([Pyramid Principle](pyramid-principle.md)) | 1-2 sentences |
| **Supporting evidence** | 3-4 key data points or arguments | 3-5 bullet points |
| **Implications** | What this means for the audience specifically | 1-2 sentences |
| **Next step / ask** | What you need from them | 1 sentence |

Total: one page maximum. Often one-half page.

## Key Principles

- **The summary IS the document for most readers** — write it as if the full report doesn't exist. If it can't stand alone, it's an introduction, not a summary.
- **Lead with the answer, not the process** — "We recommend X because Y" not "We conducted a comprehensive analysis of..."
- **Decision-focused** — frame everything around what the reader needs to DECIDE or DO
- **Numbers over adjectives** — "Retention improved 40% over 90 days" not "Retention significantly improved"
- **Write it LAST, read it FIRST** — write the full analysis first, then compress. But readers encounter the summary first.

## Common Mistakes

- **Summary of process instead of findings** — "We reviewed literature, conducted interviews, and analyzed data" (who cares? what did you FIND?)
- **Too long** — if it's 3 pages, it's a report, not a summary
- **Burying the lead** — recommendation on page 2 instead of sentence 1
- **Assumes the reader will read the full report** — they won't. The summary must stand alone.

## How To Practice

1. Take a 10-page document and write a half-page executive summary. Can someone make a decision from the summary alone?
2. Write the recommendation in one sentence. If you can't, your thinking isn't clear enough yet.
