# Coaching Techniques

## Summary

Coaching is structured support for performance improvement through questioning, reflection, and guided practice — distinct from instruction (teaching content) and mentoring (sharing experience over time). The coach helps the learner find their own answers rather than providing answers directly.

## The GROW Model (Whitmore)

| Phase | Questions | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **G — Goal** | "What do you want to achieve? What would success look like?" | Establish a clear, specific objective |
| **R — Reality** | "What's happening now? What have you tried? What's getting in the way?" | Diagnose the current situation honestly |
| **O — Options** | "What could you do? What else? What if you had no constraints?" | Generate alternatives without evaluating yet |
| **W — Will/Way forward** | "What will you do? When? What support do you need? How committed are you (1-10)?" | Commit to specific action |

## Coaching Questioning Techniques

| Technique | Example | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Open questions** | "What happened when you tried that approach?" | Encourage exploration, not yes/no |
| **Scaling** | "On a scale of 1-10, how confident are you?" | Make subjective states concrete and comparable |
| **Reframing** | "You said it 'failed' — what did you learn from it?" | Shift perspective from judgment to growth |
| **Hypothetical** | "If you knew you couldn't fail, what would you try?" | Remove constraints to surface genuine preferences |
| **Reflective** | "What does that tell you about your assumptions?" | Deepen metacognitive awareness |
| **Silence** | (Wait 5-10 seconds after asking) | Give space for thinking; resist filling the gap |

## Key Principles

- **Ask, don't tell** — the moment you give the answer, you've stopped coaching and started instructing. Both have their place, but they're different.
- **The learner owns the solution** — coaching assumes the learner has the capability; the coach's job is to unlock it through structure and questions.
- **Contract the relationship** — make coaching goals, expectations, and confidentiality explicit. Coaching without clear boundaries becomes vague mentoring.
- **Follow up** — coaching without accountability is just a nice conversation. Schedule follow-ups on commitments.
- **Pair with deliberate practice** — coaching is most powerful when it supports practice cycles: perform → reflect (with coach) → adjust → perform again.
