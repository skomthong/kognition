# Formative Assessment

## Definition

Formative assessment (Black & Wiliam, 1998) comprises all activities undertaken by teachers and students that provide information to be used as feedback to modify teaching and learning activities. Their synthesis of research evidence shows formative assessment produces substantial learning gains — among the largest effect sizes in educational intervention research.

## How To Apply It

Five key strategies (Wiliam):
1. Clarify learning intentions and criteria for success
2. Engineer effective classroom discussions, activities, and tasks that elicit evidence of learning
3. Provide feedback that moves learners forward
4. Activate students as instructional resources for one another (peer assessment)
5. Activate students as owners of their own learning (self-assessment)

## When To Use It / When Not To

**Use when:** improving learning during instruction is the goal — not just measuring it afterward. Especially powerful when combined with retrieval practice and metacognitive self-monitoring.

**Limitations:** Formative assessment requires acting on the evidence — collecting data without adjusting instruction is measurement, not formative assessment. Quality of feedback matters enormously.
