# Microlearning

## Summary

Microlearning delivers content in short, focused segments designed for constrained-resource contexts — especially adult learners with limited time, variable schedules, and mobile access needs. Systematic reviews highlight rising popularity while noting definitional and instructional design gaps. Most effective when paired with spacing, retrieval practice, and clear alignment to performance needs.

## The Science

- Based on: [Cognitive Load](../foundations/cognitive-load.md) — short segments reduce per-session load; [Cognitive Aging](../foundations/cognitive-aging.md) — shorter, repeated sessions suit older adults' processing constraints
- Related: [Retention](../foundations/retention.md) — enables distributed practice; [Habit Formation](../principles/habit-formation.md) — small enough to attach to stable daily cues

**Five Moments of Need** (Gottfredson & Mosher): Support for five workflow moments — New (learning first time), More (expanding), Apply (on the job), Solve (fixing problems), Change (adapting). Microlearning is strongest for Apply and Solve.

## How To Apply It

- **Job aids and checklists** — short reference materials at the moment of need
- **Targeted refreshers** — 3-5 minute retrieval exercises spaced over time
- **Short demos** — task-specific video or interactive walkthroughs under 5 minutes
- **Push notifications** — spaced prompts that trigger recall of key concepts
- **Mobile-first design** — respect variable connectivity, screen size, and interaction constraints

**Design guardrails:**
- Avoid fragmented concept coverage without coherence. Each micro-unit should connect to a larger learning architecture.
- Use xAPI or comparable tracking only if it serves a learning decision; govern data with privacy frameworks.
- Pair with opportunities for deeper practice, reflection, and social discussion.

## When To Use It / When Not To

**Use when:**
- Adult learners have severe time constraints and variable schedules
- The goal is reinforcement, refresher, or just-in-time performance support (Apply/Solve moments)
- Content can be meaningfully segmented without losing coherence

**Use with caution when:**
- Complex concepts require extended reasoning or synthesis
- Microlearning substitutes for rather than supplements deeper instruction
- Tracking is excessive relative to the learning decision it supports
