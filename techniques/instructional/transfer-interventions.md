# Transfer Interventions

## Summary

Transfer interventions are deliberate strategies applied BEFORE, DURING, and AFTER training to increase the likelihood that learned skills generalize to the job and are maintained over time. [Transfer of Training](../../methods/transfer-of-training.md) describes the theory; this entry covers the practical tactics.

## Intervention Timing

### Before training
| Intervention | How it works |
|---|---|
| **Manager briefing** | Manager discusses what the learner will learn and how they'll apply it. Sets expectation that application matters. |
| **Goal-setting** | Learner sets specific transfer goals BEFORE training: "I will use X technique in Y situation within 2 weeks" |
| **Readiness assessment** | Identify and address barriers: time, access, prerequisites, motivation |
| **Pre-work** | Activate prior knowledge, build schemas that instruction will extend |

### During training
| Intervention | How it works |
|---|---|
| **Realistic practice** | Practice with cues, contexts, and constraints that match the job (encoding specificity) |
| **Application planning** | Build a specific transfer plan: what, when, where, with whom |
| **Relapse prevention** | Identify likely obstacles to transfer and plan workarounds |
| **Job aids** | Create checklists, decision aids, and reference tools that go back to the job WITH the learner |

### After training
| Intervention | How it works |
|---|---|
| **Spaced retrieval boosters** | Micro-quizzes and scenario prompts at expanding intervals (1 week, 2 weeks, 1 month, 3 months) |
| **Manager follow-up** | Structured check-ins: "What did you learn? How have you applied it? What support do you need?" |
| **Peer accountability** | Learning partners or cohort groups that check in on application |
| **Coaching** | [Coaching Techniques](coaching-techniques.md) applied to specific transfer challenges |
| **Community of practice** | Ongoing community for sharing experiences, solving problems, sustaining practice |
| **Observation and feedback** | Supervisor or peer observes performance and provides targeted feedback |
| **Analytics-triggered nudges** | xAPI data triggers reminders or support when practice drops off |

## Key Principles

- **Transfer is the default failure mode** — most training fails at transfer, not at learning. Design transfer interventions as ARCHITECTURAL features, not optional extras.
- **The manager is the most powerful lever** — if the manager doesn't reinforce, support, and expect application, transfer collapses regardless of training quality.
- **Environment matters more than content** — Baldwin & Ford: trainee characteristics, training design, AND work environment all affect transfer. Fixing content without fixing environment is insufficient.
