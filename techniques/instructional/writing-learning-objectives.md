# Writing Learning Objectives

## Summary

Writing effective learning objectives is the foundational craft skill of instructional design. Objectives define what learners will be able to DO after instruction — they anchor alignment (backward design, constructive alignment), assessment design, and activity selection.

## Formats and Frameworks

### Mager's Performance Objectives
Three components:
- **Performance**: observable behavior (verb + object) — "diagnose," "calculate," "facilitate," not "understand" or "know"
- **Conditions**: circumstances under which performance occurs — "given a patient case and lab results," "without reference materials," "in a team setting"
- **Criteria**: standard of acceptable performance — "with 90% accuracy," "within 5 minutes," "meeting all safety steps"

Example: *Given a customer complaint scenario (condition), the learner will de-escalate the situation using the HEARD framework (performance) with all five steps completed in the correct sequence (criteria).*

### ABCD Format
- **Audience**: who is learning
- **Behavior**: what they'll do (observable verb)
- **Condition**: under what circumstances
- **Degree**: to what standard

### Using Bloom's Taxonomy
[Bloom's Taxonomy](blooms-taxonomy.md) provides action verbs by cognitive level:

| Level | Verbs | Example objective |
|---|---|---|
| Remember | list, define, recall, identify | List the 5 steps of the HEARD framework |
| Understand | explain, summarize, compare | Explain why de-escalation reduces complaint escalation |
| Apply | use, demonstrate, implement | Apply the HEARD framework to a simulated complaint |
| Analyze | differentiate, diagnose, compare | Analyze a complaint to determine root cause vs. surface issue |
| Evaluate | judge, assess, recommend | Evaluate whether a proposed resolution meets policy and customer needs |
| Create | design, construct, develop | Design a de-escalation protocol for a new complaint category |

## Common Mistakes

- **Vague verbs**: "understand," "know," "appreciate," "be aware of" — these aren't observable or assessable
- **Content-focused instead of performance-focused**: "Cover the compliance policy" vs. "Apply the policy to make a compliant decision"
- **Too many objectives**: if a module has 15 objectives, none of them are driving design. Focus on 2-4 that matter.
- **Mismatched Bloom level**: if the objective says "analyze" but the assessment asks learners to "list," there's misalignment
- **Forgetting conditions and criteria**: "The learner will solve problems" — under what conditions? To what standard?

## How To Practice This Skill

1. Start with the performance question: "After this, what will learners be able to DO that they couldn't before?"
2. Write the assessment first (backward design) — if you can't assess it, the objective is too vague
3. Check alignment: does the verb in the objective match the verb in the assessment?
4. Peer review: have someone else read the objective and describe what assessment they'd design for it — if they describe something different from what you intended, rewrite
