# Spaced Practice (Distributed Practice)

## Summary

Spaced practice distributes learning sessions over time rather than massing them in a single block. Cepeda et al.'s meta-analysis of hundreds of studies shows robust long-term retention benefits from spacing, making it one of the most reliable findings in learning science.

## The Science

- Based on: [Retention](../foundations/retention.md) — spaced re-encounters strengthen retrieval pathways; [Knowledge Decay Management](../foundations/knowledge-decay-management.md) — spacing counteracts the forgetting curve
- Related: [Desirable Difficulties](../foundations/desirable-difficulties.md) — spacing feels harder than massing but produces better long-term learning

## How To Apply It

- **Expanding schedule**: increase intervals between practice sessions (day 1 → day 3 → day 7 → day 14 → day 30)
- **Leitner system**: flashcard-based system where correctly answered items move to less frequent review boxes
- **Booster design**: schedule retrieval checkpoints 2-4 weeks after a workshop, then monthly
- **LMS implementation**: use quiz schedulers + calendar triggers + xAPI tracking to automate spacing
- **Pair with retrieval practice**: spacing is most effective when combined with active recall, not just re-reading

## When To Use It / When Not To

**Use when:** durable retention is the goal — compliance, certification, safety procedures, foundational knowledge. **Limitations:** optimal spacing depends on retention interval and material type; "one schedule fits all" is not supported. Learners often resist spacing because massed practice *feels* more effective.

## Sources

- Dunlosky, J., et al. (2013). Improving students' learning with effective learning techniques. *Psychological Science in the Public Interest*, 14(1), 4-58.
