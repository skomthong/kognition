# Cognitive Task Analysis (CTA)

## Definition

Cognitive task analysis focuses on the cognitive demands and expert decision processes behind complex work — capturing perceptual cues, strategies, mental models, and judgments that novices lack but experts use automatically. It goes beyond traditional task analysis (which captures steps and procedures) to capture the *thinking* behind performance.

## How To Apply It

CTA methods:
- **Critical decision method (CDM)**: interview experts about specific incidents — "Walk me through that decision. What cues did you notice? What alternatives did you consider?"
- **Think-aloud protocols**: experts narrate their reasoning while performing tasks
- **Concept mapping**: experts build visual maps of how domain concepts relate
- **Contrasting cases**: compare how experts and novices approach the same problem to identify critical differences

## When To Use It / When Not To

**Use when:** designing for complex judgment work — medical diagnosis, financial analysis, leadership decisions, cybersecurity, troubleshooting. CTA reveals what makes expert performance different from novice performance, which directly informs scenario design, simulation design, and training focus.

**Limitations:** time-intensive; needs skilled interviewer. Experts often can't articulate their tacit knowledge without structured elicitation. Results must be translated into instructional designs (scenarios, worked examples, decision aids).
