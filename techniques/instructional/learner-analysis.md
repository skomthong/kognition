# Learner Analysis

## Definition

Learner analysis is a structured process for describing who adult learners are — current competence, prior knowledge, experience, motivation, constraints — so instruction can be targeted, equitable, and efficient. In systems-oriented instructional design, learner analysis is an explicit stage that informs performance objectives and assessments.

## How To Apply It

- **Diagnose prior knowledge**: concept inventories, pretests, confidence ratings to detect misconceptions and calibration gaps
- **Assess constraints**: time availability, technology access, schedule variability, role demands
- **Segment by decision patterns**: CTA (Cognitive Task Analysis) reveals different mental models and cue usage across experience levels — more useful than demographics alone
- **Assess transfer climate**: identify where the organization will support or block application (LTSI)
- **Triangulate**: combine self-report, performance data, and observation rather than relying on surveys alone

## When To Use It / When Not To

**Use when:** stakes are high, learners are diverse, or transfer is critical. **Limitation:** analysis paralysis — collecting data you cannot act on. When the problem is well-defined, validate through rapid prototypes and pilots instead.
