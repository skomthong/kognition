# Personalized Learning

## Definition

Personalized learning tailors the pace, path, content, and/or environment of learning to individual learner needs, goals, prior knowledge, and preferences. It ranges from low-tech differentiation (instructor-led pathway adjustments) to high-tech adaptive systems (algorithm-driven content sequencing).

## How It Works

Four dimensions of personalization:

| Dimension | What varies | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Pace** | Speed of progression | Self-paced modules, competency-based advancement, flexible deadlines |
| **Path** | Sequence and route | Branching pathways, elective modules, learner-chosen case topics |
| **Content** | Materials and resources | Difficulty-adjusted problems, role-specific scenarios, language/modality options |
| **Environment** | Context and support | Choice of sync/async, individual/group, location, support level |

## Key Principles

- Personalization is not just "more choice" — it requires diagnostic data (learner analysis, pretests, learning analytics) to make informed adjustments.
- **Adaptive learning** uses algorithms to adjust content difficulty, sequencing, or feedback based on learner performance in real time. Effective but requires careful design to avoid opaque recommendations and over-reliance on engagement proxies.
- Keep learning goals constant while varying the path — personalization of means, not ends.
- Adults are especially receptive to personalization because their prior knowledge varies widely and time is scarce — skip what they already know, focus where they need growth.
- Personalization at scale requires system-layer support: LMS/LXP pathways, xAPI tracking, analytics dashboards.
- Privacy risk increases with personalization — more data collection means more governance needed (NIST PF).

## Levels of Personalization

| Level | Mechanism | Technology requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **Instructor-led** | Facilitator adjusts based on observation and formative assessment | Low — human judgment |
| **Rule-based** | Pre-defined branching paths based on pretest or performance thresholds | Medium — LMS branching |
| **Adaptive** | Algorithm adjusts in real time based on performance patterns | High — adaptive platform |
| **AI-driven** | Generative AI provides individualized feedback, explanations, practice | High — AI integration, governance |

## When To Use It / When Not To

**Use when:** learner populations have diverse prior knowledge, time is constrained, and the system can collect and act on diagnostic data. Especially valuable in competency-based and workplace learning contexts.

**Limitations:** can become opaque ("why am I seeing this?") without transparency. Over-personalization can fragment shared learning experiences and undermine cohort identity. Personalization based on "learning styles" is not supported by evidence — personalize based on prior knowledge, performance, and goals, not style preferences.
